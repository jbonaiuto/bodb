Acknowledgements

The Brain Operation Database (BODB) is part of the University of Southern California Brain Project (USCBP) and has been designed and implemented by many people under the leadership of Michael A. Arbib. Mihail Bota designed the related NeuroHomology Database (NHDB). Salvador Marmol designed ARDB (the Action Recognition Database), the precursor of BODB, and Rishabh Tandon updated it and released ARDB version 2.0. The name BODB was chosen to replace ARDB to better indicate the wide applicability of the systemâ€™s functionalities. We also appreciate the generosity of Peter Fox and Jack Lancaster from the Research Imaging Center, The University of Texas Health Science Center at San Antonio, for making available the brain templates and database of regional Talairach coordinates from their Talairach Demon which was developed as part of BrainMap an online database of published functional neuroimaging experiments with coordinate-based (Talairach) activation locations. Anon Plangprasopchok took the lead in implementing version 2.0 of BODB while Nantana Tinroongroj developed the first version of the Talairach Demon now integrated with BODB. Implementation of version 3.0 of BODB was primarily the work of James Bonaiuto.

Current work on this Website is based in part upon work supported by the National Science Foundation under Grant No. 0924674 (Michael A. Arbib, Principal Investigator).
